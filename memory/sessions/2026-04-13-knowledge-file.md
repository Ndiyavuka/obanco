## Sleep & Evening Routine

**Target bedtime: 9:40 PM** (for 8 hours before 5:40 AM wake-up)
- Mitch gets best sleep with 8 hours; anything less, he notices cognitive and physical decline
- Body naturally wants to stay up late (extroverted, stimulation-seeking)
- Chronic challenge: pressure to sleep before body is ready

**Evening sequence (roughly 45 minutes total before bed):**
1. Shower (~10 min) — physiological reset via cold-then-warm cycle, air-drying, then warm clothes
2. Floss (~5 min)
3. Brush teeth (~5 min)
4. Clean kitty litter (~5 min)
5. Feed cats (~5 min)
6. Bag prep & clothes for next day (variable)
7. Variable time for bathroom use (sometimes significantly longer than typical)

**Sleep anxiety pattern:**
- Gets stressed about time crunches ("I have an hour but this takes 90 minutes")
- Anxiety keeps him wired, so he stays up to finish everything rather than go to bed with tasks undone
- Lying in bed with unfinished items is worse than missing sleep
- Writing things down to handle tomorrow helps sometimes

**Food timing:**
- No eating in last 2 hours before bed (so by 7:40 PM if bedtime is 9:40 PM)
- When he eats closer to bedtime, heart rate stays elevated (60–65 bpm) and he can't sleep
- With proper gap, heart rate drops to 55 or below and body feels calm enough to sleep
- Big dinners in late afternoon/early evening (4:00–7:00 PM) help satisfy hunger and prevent late snacking
- Dinner tonight: large Crockpot portion with yogurt & cayenne; small snack of 72% cacao chips afterward

**Sleep aids (used strategically, not daily):**
- Magnesium: helps with muscle recovery, take after exercise
- Magnesium: helps with muscle recovery, take after exercise
- Trazodone: makes body heavy, longer duration through night
- Melatonin: initiates drowsiness, shorter duration
- Valerian root: occasional, used when needing significant help
- When really off-schedule (like tonight after 2:30 AM bedtime last night): combines trazodone + melatonin together, taken ~30 min before target bedtime

**Break-time rest:**
- Sometimes uses work breaks (30-min) to nap on yoga mat in dark room (very refreshing)
- Recently replaced this with AI/LLM study, which keeps him wired — not helpful for afternoon energy

**Cascade failure pattern:**
- Late night → undersleep → early start → afternoon crash (vulnerable window, mid-to-late post-work) → missed exercise → suboptimal everything downstream
