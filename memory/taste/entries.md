id: open-andre-agassi-2009
title: Open
format: book
creator: André Agassi (with J.R. Moehringer)
period_of_release: 2009
period_consumed: 2026 (recently completed)
---
Agassi's account of his relationship with his father's abuse and his tendency to falsify what he feels—saying he hates tennis, being corrected by others, then realizing he can't even trust his own internal narrative. What struck Mitch most was the dissonance between what Agassi claimed and what he actually felt, the fracture between the story he told himself and the reality he lived. Mitch felt compassion for that fracture. The book is well-structured and coherent overall, though Mitch found it occasionally over-dramatized—moments dilated for suspense that didn't need it. But the essential content, the real arc, held.

id: stevie-wonder-1972
title: Stevie Wonder [various works]
format: music
creator: Stevie Wonder
period_of_release: 1960s–present
period_consumed: lifetime (ongoing)
---
Mitch's closest thing to an all-time favorite artist. What defines Wonder for him: infinite creativity within a coherent vision. His music draws from jazz, blues, soul, but he's not confined to any—he experiments. Lyrically dense and meaningful. Harmonically sophisticated but never recherché; the complexity never alienates. Mitch has absolute pitch and tracks harmonic structure directly. In Wonder he hears a mastery of compression without loss—density without opacity. The metaphor Mitch uses: "leaves and flowers in the sky, but roots deep in the earth." You never lose the thread of the song's evolution, even as it gets complex. Wonder achieves what effective LLMs achieve: something so intricate it doesn't seem inaccessible. Key tracks: Sir Duke, If It's Magic, Isn't She Lovely, Summer Soft, Another Star, Tuesday Heartbreak, Part-Time Lover, Cash in Your Face, You've Got It Bad Girl, Rocket Love, Fun Day.

id: the-ezra-klein-show
title: The Ezra Klein Show
format: podcast
creator: Ezra Klein
period_of_release: 2015–present
period_consumed: ongoing, primary source
---
Mitch's main pillar for staying informed on current affairs in a non-ephemeral way. What draws him: Ezra's tendency to enter into dialogue with people politically opposed to him or significantly disagreeing with him. The structure of the show appeals to Mitch more than any single episode—two intelligences that don't align, meeting, and thought constructing itself through the disagreement. No easy resolution, just clarity of conflict. Ezra simplifies the complex without denying complexity; he enters into real difficulty and emerges with a path you can follow. The show serves dual purposes: staying informed and modeling a dialogic form of thinking that mirrors Mitch's own collaborative intellectual style.

format: album / track
creator: Googoosh (Faegheh Atashin)
period_of_release: 2008
period_consumed: 2000s
---
It communicates painful sadness really well. Googoosh has a strong voice — even when it's soft, it's got presence. The tension of the F minor 3rd bordering with the 9th, and that interval recurring at other points in the song, gives it a sense of intense almost-urgency, like the feeling is hot and throbs. It's the song itself that stands out, not the artist as a whole.
